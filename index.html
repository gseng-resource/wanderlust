Task 1: Vintage Luxury Watch/Goods Shops - Shinjuku Area

1. BEST VINTAGE SHINJUKU

Address: 3-17-12 B1F, Shinjuku, Shinjuku-ku, Tokyo, 160-0022

Operating Hours: Mon-Fri: 12:00 PM - 8:00 PM; Weekends & Holidays: 11:00 AM - 8:00 PM

View on Google Maps

Reviews:

Positive:

  • Excellent selection of vintage Rolex, very knowledgeable staff, and great service.
  • Fantastic range of watches, and the prices are very competitive for the quality.
  • Friendly staff who speak English and Chinese, offering tax-free shopping.

Negative:

  • Some pieces felt slightly overpriced compared to other vintage shops.
  • Store can get quite busy, long wait for assistance.
  • Limited negotiation on prices, common in Japan but a slight disappointment.

2. Quark Shinjuku Store

Address: 3-17-7 Shinjuku, Shinjuku-ku, Tokyo, 160-0022

Operating Hours: Generally 11:00 AM - 7:30 PM (Verify daily)

View on Google Maps

Reviews:

Positive:

  • The go-to for Rolex in Japan; massive selection and professional staff.
  • Great experience and hospitality; will definitely return.
  • Fantastic & efficient services; will certainly visit again.

Negative:

  • Prices can be on the higher end, as expected from a major dealer.
  • Store can be overwhelming with so many watches; easy to feel rushed.
  • Some older pieces might not have detailed provenance.

3. Daikokuya Shinjuku Main Store

Address: 3-23-14 Shinjuku, Shinjuku-ku, Tokyo, 160-0022

Operating Hours: 10:30 AM - 7:30 PM (Daily)

View on Google Maps

Reviews:

Positive:

  • Great place for both vintage watches and luxury bags; found a beautiful pre-owned Chanel.
  • Good selection and fair prices; helpful staff despite language barrier.
  • Reliable and trustworthy; satisfied with multiple purchases.

Negative:

  • Store can feel a bit cluttered; items not always elegantly displayed.
  • Finding truly rare vintage pieces can be hit or miss.
  • Customer service can be less personalized than specialty stores.

4. Komehyo Shinjuku Store

Address: 3-5-6 Shinjuku, Shinjuku-ku, Tokyo, 160-0022

Operating Hours: 11:00 AM - 8:00 PM (Daily)

View on Google Maps

Reviews:

Positive:

  • Huge multi-floor store with incredible inventory of pre-owned luxury goods.
  • Items are well-inspected and authenticated, providing peace of mind.
  • Tax-free shopping available and staff are generally helpful.

Negative:

  • Can be very crowded, especially on weekends, making browsing difficult.
  • Some items are priced quite high; better deals might be found elsewhere.
  • Sheer volume of items can be overwhelming, not a boutique experience.

5. Ginza Rasin Shinjuku Branch

Address: 3-17-7 Shinjuku, Shinjuku-ku, Tokyo, 160-0022

Operating Hours: 11:00 AM - 8:00 PM (Daily)

View on Google Maps

Reviews:

Positive:

  • Excellent selection of pre-owned Rolex and other high-end watches.
  • Smooth and efficient purchase process; very happy with the watch.
  • Good reputation and transparent pricing; confident in their authentication.

Negative:

  • Prices can be firm and on the higher side for certain models.
  • Store can be quite busy; not a leisurely browsing experience.
  • Focus is heavily on watches; not ideal for other luxury goods.

Task 2: Vintage Luxury Watch/Goods Shops - Other Tokyo Areas

1. Jack Road (Nakano Broadway)

Address: 5-52-15 Nakano, Nakano-ku, Tokyo 164-0001 (Inside Nakano Broadway)

Operating Hours: 11:00 AM - 8:00 PM (Daily)

View on Google Maps

Reviews:

Positive:

  • Legendary for watches; immense selection from modern to rare vintage.
  • Personalized purchasing experience in a private area is a nice touch.
  • Highly knowledgeable and patient staff; competitive prices.

Negative:

  • Nakano Broadway can be overwhelming and crowded.
  • Highly sought-after vintage models sell out quickly due to popularity.
  • Some truly rare pieces command a premium.

2. Betty Road (Nakano Broadway)

Address: 5-52-15 Nakano, Nakano-ku, Tokyo 164-0001 (Inside Nakano Broadway)

Operating Hours: 11:00 AM - 8:00 PM (Daily)

View on Google Maps

Reviews:

Positive:

  • Fantastic selection of vintage luxury handbags and women's watches.
  • Staff are very attentive and helpful in finding specific styles.
  • Great place for unique vintage jewelry.

Negative:

  • Can be overshadowed by Jack Road's watch focus.
  • Prices for very rare vintage bags can be quite high.
  • Niche focus, not for those not looking for women's specific luxury items.

3. Firekids (Nakano Broadway)

Address: 5-52-15 Nakano, Nakano-ku, Tokyo 164-0001 (Inside Nakano Broadway)

Operating Hours: Generally 11:00 AM - 7:00 PM (Hours can vary, check locally)

View on Google Maps

Reviews:

Positive:

  • Treasure trove for unique and lesser-known vintage watches.
  • Passionate and knowledgeable staff, happy to talk watches without pressure.
  • Many pieces thoughtfully refurbished, offering great value.

Negative:

  • Selection is niche, not for those only looking for mainstream Rolex.
  • Store can be small and feel a bit cramped when busy.
  • May require a deeper understanding of vintage watches to fully appreciate.

4. Corleone Vintage (Harajuku)

Address: 3-23-2 Jingumae, Shibuya-ku, Tokyo, 150-0001

Operating Hours: Typically 12:00 PM - 7:00 PM (Closed Wednesdays - verify)

View on Google Maps

Reviews:

Positive:

  • Best place for vintage Rolex and Tudor; Snowflake Submariners are a highlight.
  • Meticulously curated selection; every piece is a gem.
  • Knowledgeable staff and excellent service; confident in authenticity.

Negative:

  • Selection is not as vast as larger stores like Jack Road.
  • Prices are on the higher end, reflecting quality and rarity.
  • Can be a bit out of the way compared to more central shopping districts.

5. Cottontail (Taito)

Address: 3-1-10 Taito, Taito-ku, Tokyo, 110-0016

Operating Hours: Hours vary, often by appointment or limited days (Check ahead)

View on Google Maps

Reviews:

Positive:

  • Truly unique selection of vintage watches, focusing on rare and underappreciated pieces.
  • Owner is incredibly passionate and provides intimate, personalized experience.
  • Perfect for discovering something out of the ordinary and appreciating horological history.

Negative:

  • Located in Taito, less central for general shopping, requiring a dedicated trip.
  • Very small inventory compared to larger stores.
  • May require specific knowledge or interest in niche vintage watches.

Task 4: Route Planning

Route A: Vintage Focus

Selected Shops:

  • BEST VINTAGE SHINJUKU
  • Quark Shinjuku Store
  • Daikokuya Shinjuku Main Store
  • Jack Road (Nakano Broadway)
  • Betty Road (Nakano Broadway)
  • Firekids (Nakano Broadway)

Route Explanation: This route focuses entirely on vintage luxury goods. We'll start in Shinjuku, visiting the three key vintage shops there, which are all relatively close to each other. Then, we'll head to Nakano, where Jack Road, Betty Road, and Firekids are all located within the same Nakano Broadway complex, making it efficient to visit all three.

View Route A on Google Maps

Route B: Mixed Shopping

Selected Shops:

  • BEST VINTAGE SHINJUKU
  • Quark Shinjuku Store
  • Komehyo Shinjuku Store
  • ON Flagship Store Tokyo Cat Street
  • Lululemon Omotesando
  • Nintendo Tokyo

Route Explanation: This route starts with three vintage luxury shops in Shinjuku, then transitions to a lifestyle shopping experience in the Shibuya/Omotesando area. The travel between Shinjuku and Shibuya is best done by train (JR Yamanote Line). ON, Lululemon, and Nintendo Tokyo are relatively close to each other in the Shibuya/Omotesando/Cat Street vicinity.

Route C: Full Day Itinerary (9 Shops)

Selected Shops:

  • Vintage (Task 1): BEST VINTAGE SHINJUKU, Quark Shinjuku Store, Komehyo Shinjuku Store
  • Vintage (Task 2): Jack Road (Nakano Broadway), Betty Road (Nakano Broadway), Corleone Vintage (Harajuku)
  • Lifestyle (Task 3): ON Flagship Store Tokyo Cat Street, Nintendo Tokyo, Montbell Shibuya

Start Time: 10:00 AM

Total Duration: 10 hours

Time per Store: 20 minutes

Step-by-step breakdown:

  1. 10:00 AM - 10:20 AM: BEST VINTAGE SHINJUKU (Walk from Hotel Gracery Shinjuku, approx. 10-15 min)
  2. 10:20 AM - 10:40 AM: Quark Shinjuku Store (Walk from BEST VINTAGE, approx. 2 min)
  3. 10:40 AM - 11:00 AM: Komehyo Shinjuku Store (Walk from Quark, approx. 5-7 min)
  4. 11:00 AM - 11:30 AM: Travel to Nakano (Walk to Shinjuku Station, JR Chuo Line to Nakano, walk to Nakano Broadway - approx. 25-30 min total)
  5. 11:30 AM - 11:50 AM: Jack Road (Nakano Broadway)
  6. 11:50 AM - 12:10 PM: Betty Road (Nakano Broadway)
  7. 12:10 PM - 1:00 PM: Lunch Break in Nakano
  8. 1:00 PM - 1:45 PM: Travel to Harajuku/Shibuya (Walk to Nakano Station, JR Chuo Line to Shinjuku, transfer to JR Yamanote Line to Harajuku, walk to Corleone Vintage - approx. 35-45 min total)
  9. 1:45 PM - 2:05 PM: Corleone Vintage (Harajuku)
  10. 2:05 PM - 2:20 PM: Travel to Cat Street/Omotesando area (Walk to ON Flagship Store, approx. 5-10 min)
  11. 2:20 PM - 2:40 PM: ON Flagship Store Tokyo Cat Street
  12. 2:40 PM - 3:00 PM: Lululemon Omotesando (Walk from ON, approx. 5-7 min)
  13. 3:00 PM - 3:20 PM: Travel to Shibuya PARCO (Walk to Shibuya PARCO, approx. 10-15 min)
  14. 3:20 PM - 3:40 PM: Nintendo Tokyo
  15. 3:40 PM - 4:00 PM: Montbell Shibuya (Walk from Shibuya PARCO, approx. 5-7 min)
  16. **4:00 PM - 8:00 PM: Free time/further exploration/dinner**
